# Genre Bucket: mystery (本格 + 社会派)

> Status: **planned for v0.4** · placeholder directory.

This bucket will host case studies in the mystery genre family, covering two sub-traditions:

- **本格** (puzzle / fair-play mystery) — clue economy, suspect graphs, reader-detective satisfaction
- **社会派** (social-critique mystery) — crime as social X-ray, structural causation, motive over puzzle

These are different enough to merit separate cases within this bucket. Both fall under "mystery" as the umbrella genre per [`DECISIONS.md`](../../../DECISIONS.md) D10.

## What v0.4 will deliver

- `case-{tbd-title}-honkaku.md` — case study for a 本格-style work
- `case-{tbd-title}-shakai.md` — case study for a 社会派-style work
- Genre-adaptation entries in [`docs/04-genre-adaptation.md`](../../../docs/04-genre-adaptation.md) "SOUL persona tuning" table (mystery column)
- Forbidden patterns specific to mystery (e.g., "withhold clue from reader" for 本格; "diagnose the social structure too explicitly" for 社会派)

## What this directory does NOT include yet

- Case study skeletons. Lands in v0.4 when the author begins drafting in this genre family.
- Workflow adaptation specifics — those stay in `docs/04-genre-adaptation.md` until enough mystery work exists to extract reusable patterns from production runs (not from prior).

## Why two sub-traditions in one bucket

In the **bucket-of-three** constraint (per D10, hiring managers won't scroll past 3), grouping 本格 + 社会派 reflects a real shared infrastructure — both depend on causality being legible, motive being earnable, and disclosure pacing being controlled. The differences are in **what counts as legible** (puzzle logic vs social logic) and **what counts as earned** (clue revelation vs structural revelation).

A separate bucket for each would be the academic-completeness move; one bucket with two cases is the PM-portfolio move.

## Reading order (once v0.4 lands)

1. `case-honkaku.md` first — fair-play structure exposes SOUL adaptations cleanly because the rules are crisp
2. `case-shakai.md` second — different adaptation pattern (motive-first, not clue-first), different forbidden list
